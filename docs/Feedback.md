12. Blockchain & Decentralized Technologies

For most NGOs, blockchain should complement, not replace, traditional finance.

Potential use cases include:

Smart contracts for milestone-based grant disbursements.
Tamper-evident audit trails using hashed transaction records.
Digital identities for beneficiaries and partners.
Tokenized vouchers for cash or assistance programs where regulations permit.
Cross-border settlement where legally compliant and operationally beneficial.

Traditional accounting, banking, and regulatory compliance will still remain the system of record for the foreseeable future.

My recommendation for your IMS

Given the scope of your platform (finance, grants, projects, procurement, HR, logistics, MEAL, GIS, and multilingual support across multiple countries), I would avoid trying to emulate SAP or Dynamics feature-for-feature. Instead, position your IMS as an AI-first Humanitarian ERP purpose-built for international NGOs, UN agencies, and development organizations.

That means designing around grants rather than departments, projects rather than cost centers, real-time decisions rather than historical reports, and AI agents that collaborate across finance, operations, compliance, and MEAL. With a robust event-driven architecture, unified financial data model, and open APIs, the platform would be well positioned to support future technologies—including blockchain where appropriate—without making them central to the system.

I think this could become one of the most comprehensive NGO-focused ERP platforms available, provided the architecture remains modular, standards-based, and built around a single source of truth.
